Fandom

Fans are, or their media representation by non-fans are, often “a practice of uncontrolled, socially unacceptable desire” to many people (Stanfill). However, approaching fans as media audiences, scholars often see fans “as empowered through their fandom to have more control over their media experience” since they can be productive and engage with mediated elements actively (Stanfill). In other words, instead of being passive information receivers, fans can perform as active participants in this mediated community.

K-pop

In the context of K-pop--a highly-systemized entertainment industry, the media element that fans are actively engaged with is "idol". Idol is a project designed elaborately by the Korean management company from scratch to attract consumers in the market. The companies often control every aspect of idols, not only professional skills like dancing or singing but also there own personal life “that take[s] place in the public eye” to meet the taste of fans (Lee and Nornes). This “idol-as-commodity” model determines that fans play a crucial role in the industry.

This project

By analyzing and visualizing data from fan-created contents uploaded on YouTube, this project tries to demonstrate how different K-pop groups may attract different types of fans and, in return, how those fans help to establish a digital representation of K-pop groups through their spontaneously produced contents.
